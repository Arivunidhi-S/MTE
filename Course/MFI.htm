METALLURGICAL FAILURE INVESTIGATION

This two day course aims to equip participants with a broad background knowledge of failure investigation techniques and procedures

• The course has an emphasis on metallic materials but in varied industries e.g. Oil & Gas, Aerospace and

Railways so participants and can leverage on parallels

• Instruction will be given by lectures, interactive discussion, case studies and practical demonstration

• To have an overall view of the techniques and    2 Days procedures involved in Failure Investigation

• To       understand        site         preparation,       on-site metallography and sample extraction

• To illustrate methodologies for analysis of data Certificate of Attendance

• To identify different failure modes

 

Course Contents

• The standard procedures of an investigation

•  The modes of failure including; the four fracture modes e.g. Dimple Rupture, cleavage, fatigue and   decohesive  rupture   and   their   various mechanisms, corrosion, environmentally assisted cracking e.g. chloride stress corrosion cracking, sensitization, hydrogen embrittlement Methodologies of data analysis

• Case studies

• On-site metallography and replication

 

Who Should Attend

Engineers from all industries particularly Oil & Gas, Aerospace and Railways

Material Scientists and Engineers

Project Engineers

Asset Integrity Engineers

 

Course Duration

2 days
